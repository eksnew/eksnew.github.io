Sunless Sea 词汇笔记（一）

Posted on 2024-03-20 Edited on 2024-03-21

《无光之海(Sunless Sea)》是文本量达百万的角色扮演游戏，以其剧本及设定闻名。 因文本难度高且无中文，又被称为《雅思之海》。

以我贫瘠的词汇量看，这款游戏可能要玩几年，故现开一栏专门用于记录词汇。为了更好地理解这些词汇的意思，我会大量借助GPT，好处是其中可能会包含相关的词汇考究。计划每天临走前玩半小时，就当学英语了。

1. London!

1.1. Adopt a Blemmigan Gallivanter

  • Blemmigan Gallivanter
    • gallivant. An informal verb that means go around from one place to another in the pursuit of pleasure or entertainment.
    • Gallivanter.

Blemmigan是什么？

  • A Blemmigan sits atop a splintered crate ...
    • atop. [preposition]. on the top of.
    • splintered. splinter. [verb]. break or cause to break into small sharp fragments.
    • crate. [noun]. a slatted wooden case used for transporting or storing goods.

1.1.1. A polite bow

  • You extend your hand and the Blemmigan leaps on. Its tendrils entwine with your fingers, and it chitters quietly. It meets your gaze and bobs its dome in greeting. It's very small - is it old enough for naval service? You're pretty sure its moustache has been drawn on in pen, in an attempt to look older.
    • leaps on. [Verb phrase]. "Leaps" is the base form of the verb, while "on" is a preposition. Together, they form the phrasal verb "leaps on," meaning to jump or spring onto something eagerly or suddenly.
    • tendril. [Noun]. Tendrils are slender, elongated, or threadlike appendages or extensions. They often refer to plant structures that coil around objects for support, but in this context, they likely describe similar appendages on the creature.
    • entwine. [Verb]. "Entwine" is a verb that means to twist or interlace something with another thing, typically in a way that forms a complex pattern. In this sentence, the tendrils of the creature are described as entwining with the fingers of the person.
    • chitters. [Verb]. "Chitters" is the third-person singular form of the verb "chitter." It refers to the sound produced by small animals or insects, typically characterized by short, high-pitched sounds.
    • gaze. [Noun]. "Gaze" refers to a steady or intent look, often indicating interest, curiosity, or admiration.
    • bobs. [Verb]. "Bobs" is the third-person singular form of the verb "bob." It means to move up and down quickly and repeatedly, often in a rhythmic or jerky manner.
    • dome. [Noun]. A dome is a rounded or curved structure, typically forming the roof or ceiling of a building or structure. In this context, it likely describes the head or body of the Blemmigan, which is being observed by the person.
    • naval. [Adjective]. "Naval" relates to a navy or maritime matters, typically referring to activities or operations conducted by a navy.
    • moustache. [Noun]. Also spelled "mustache." A moustache is facial hair that grows on the upper lip of a person, typically worn by men.
  • As you approach your ship's gangplank, the creature flutes and vibrates with apparent happiness. The bosun winces as you bring it aboard.
    • gangplank. [Noun]. A gangplank is a movable plank or ramp that connects a ship to a pier or another ship, allowing people to board or disembark.
    • flutes. [Verb]. In this context, "flutes" is used as a verb to describe the action of the creature making a musical or melodious sound, often produced by blowing across a hole or aperture. It suggests that the creature is emitting a pleasant or happy noise.
    • bosun. [Noun]. Also spelled "boatswain." A bosun is a warrant officer or petty officer on a merchant ship or naval vessel, responsible for the maintenance of the ship and its equipment. In this context, the bosun seems to be a crew member aboard the ship.
    • winces. [Verb]. "Winces" is the third-person singular form of the verb "wince." It means to flinch slightly or involuntarily in response to pain, discomfort, or a strong emotion. In this sentence, the bosun winces, suggesting that they react with discomfort or unease as the creature is brought aboard the ship.

1.2. Visit the Admiralty's Survey Office

  • The Admiralty's picket fleets and intelligence networks are long gone. The Survey Office pays zee-captains a small sum for recent information.
    • admiralty. [Proper noun]. In this context, "Admiralty" likely refers to a government department or authority responsible for naval affairs, such as the British Admiralty.
    • picket. [Noun]. In this context, "picket" may refer to a small group of ships or vessels stationed for surveillance or patrolling purposes. It could also imply a group of naval vessels assigned to guard a particular area or to perform a specific duty.
    • sum. [Noun]. "Sum" refers to a particular amount of money or a quantity of something. In this context, it suggests that the Survey Office compensates zee-captains with a modest payment in exchange for recent information.
  • The first time you trade in a Port Report, you'll gain Admiralty's Favour.
    • favour. [Noun]. In this context, "Favour" refers to approval, support, or goodwill granted by the Admiralty. It indicates a positive relationship or standing with the Admiralty, possibly leading to benefits or privileges in future interactions.

    • assured. [Adjective]. In this context, "assured" means guaranteed or confident. It suggests that if the speaker can be confident or guaranteed of something, they are willing to proceed with the conversation or action.
    • discretion. [Noun]. "Discretion" refers to the quality of being discreet or careful in one's actions or speech, especially to avoid causing offense or revealing confidential information. In this context, the speaker is likely asking for assurance that the listener will handle the information or situation with discretion and confidentiality.

  • "This port - ""Speak to our agent there, and return. We'll see that you're rewarded."

    • retrieve. [Verb]. "Retrieve" means to bring or get something back, especially from a distant or inaccessible place. In this context, it suggests the action of obtaining strategic information from the Iron & Misery Company Funging Station.
    • misery. [Noun]. A state or feeling of great distress or discomfort of mind or body. In this context, "Misery" likely refers to a proper noun, possibly the name of a company or location.
    • funging. [Noun]. The word "funging" appears to be a specialized term related to the activity or operation conducted at the Iron & Misery Company Funging Station. Without additional context, its precise meaning is unclear, but it likely refers to a specific task or process associated with the station.

  • Done. Out into the foggy street. Turn your collar up.
